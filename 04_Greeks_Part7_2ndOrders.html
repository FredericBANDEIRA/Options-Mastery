Volga (Vomma) — Convexity in Vol Space
Definition
\[\text{Volga} = \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial \sigma^2} = \frac{\partial \nu}{\partial \sigma} = \nu \cdot \frac{d_1 d_2}{\sigma}\]
What it is
Vol's Gamma
Vega is the slope in vol space; Volga is the curvature. Analogous to how Gamma is the curvature in spot space.
Sign driver
Sign of d₁·d₂
Volga > 0 when d₁·d₂ > 0 (wings). Near ATM, d₁·d₂ can be negative → Volga ≈ 0 or slightly negative.
P&L formula
½·Volga·(Δσ)²
Non-linear vol risk — critical for large vol moves and stress scenarios.
The Double-Hump Profile
100 20% 1.0
ATM — Volga ≈ 0 (slightly negative)

Near the strike, d₁ and d₂ have opposite signs — their product is negative (or small positive). Higher vol flattens the Vega peak but doesn't add much more convexity. ATM Vega is nearly linear in vol.

Wings (OTM/ITM) — Volga > 0

Away from the strike, d₁·d₂ > 0. A small increase in vol dramatically increases the probability that these "far-from-ATM" options could finish ITM, creating significant convexity in vol. This is why OTM options are "Volga plays."

Long vs Short Volga — Convexity in Vol Space
Long Volga — Positive Convexity

Your Vega increases when vol rises. To stay Vega-neutral, you sell vol (sell high). When vol falls, Vega decreases → you buy vol (buy low). Systematic "vol scalping" — sell high, buy low in vol space.

Short Volga — Negative Convexity

Your Vega decreases when vol rises → forced to buy vol high. When vol falls, Vega increases → sell vol low. Systematic "vol anti-scalping" — buy high, sell low. The losing side of vol convexity.

P&L Approximation & Vol-of-Vol
Complete Vol P&L Including Convexity
\[\Delta V \approx \underbrace{\nu \cdot \Delta\sigma}_{\text{linear Vega P\&L}} + \underbrace{\frac{1}{2}\,\text{Volga}\cdot(\Delta\sigma)^2}_{\text{non-linear convexity}}\]
⚡ When Volga dominates: During large vol moves (crisis, vol crush), the (Δσ)² term grows quadratically. If vol jumps 5 points (Δσ = 0.05) and Volga = 200, the convexity P&L = ½ × 200 × 0.0025 = $0.25 — which can dwarf the linear Vega P&L on short-dated OTM options. This is why wing options profit disproportionately during vol spikes.
🎲 Vol-of-Vol connection: Volga is the primary measure of exposure to stochastic volatility — vol-of-vol. Even if Δσ has zero mean (vol is mean-reverting), a non-zero vol-of-vol generates systematic Volga P&L. This is why Heston and rough vol models, which capture vol clustering, produce different Volga profiles than Black-Scholes.

Vanna — The Skew Machine
Definition — Two Equivalent Interpretations
\[\text{Vanna} = \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S\,\partial\sigma} = \underbrace{\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial S}}_{\text{Vega's spot sensitivity}} = \underbrace{\frac{\partial \Delta}{\partial \sigma}}_{\text{Delta's vol sensitivity}}\]
As ∂ν/∂S
How vol exposure shifts as spot moves
When the stock rallies, does your Vega increase or decrease?
As ∂Δ/∂σ
How hedge ratio shifts as vol changes
When IV spikes, does your Delta go up or down? (The smile-adjusted delta from module 3)
💡 Key property: Call Vanna = Put Vanna (same strike, same maturity). Both have identical Vega → identical ∂ν/∂S. This is a direct consequence of put-call parity.
The Vanna Profile — Slope of Vega

Vanna is essentially the derivative (slope) of the Vega bell curve. Since Vega peaks at ATM, the slope (Vanna) is zero at ATM and changes sign on either side:

OTM Calls / ITM Puts (S < K)
Positive Vanna
d₂ < 0. As vol rises, OTM call Delta increases (more likely to go ITM). As spot rises, Vega increases (approaching ATM).
ATM (S ≈ K)
Vanna ≈ 0
At the Vega peak. The slope of the bell curve is zero at the maximum.
ITM Calls / OTM Puts (S > K)
Negative Vanna
d₂ > 0. As vol rises, ITM call Delta decreases (less certain to stay ITM). As spot falls toward K, Vega increases.

Spot-Vol Correlation — The Equity Skew Connection

In equity markets, spot and vol are negatively correlated: when the market falls, IV spikes. This creates the famous equity volatility skew.

\[\text{Vanna P\&L} = \text{Vanna} \times \Delta S \times \Delta\sigma\]
Long Vanna
Profitable when ΔS·Δσ > 0
Same-direction moves in spot and vol. In equities (negative ρ_SV), this means SHORT positions (spot down, vol up).
Short Vanna
Profitable when ΔS·Δσ < 0
Opposite-direction moves. Typical for long equity positions with hedged vol.
The Risk Reversal Trade — No Free Lunch

A common interview question: "Can you harvest the Vanna P&L in equity markets by buying OTM puts and selling OTM calls (Long Risk Reversal)?"

The temptation
  • OTM puts have positive Vanna P&L in a crash (spot ↓, vol ↑)
  • OTM calls have negative Vanna P&L
  • Long risk reversal = long put Vanna → sounds free
The "Skew Theta" offset
  • OTM puts have higher IV than OTM calls (equity skew)
  • Higher IV → higher Theta on the long put
  • "Skew Theta" creates negative daily carry that offsets expected Vanna P&L
🚩 No free lunch: The skew exists precisely because of the negative spot-vol correlation. The market has already priced the Vanna P&L into the skew via higher put IV. You pay for this in Theta every day. The expected Vanna gain = the Skew Theta cost. To profit from Vanna, you need your view on vol correlation to be better than the market's.
Charm — Delta's Time Decay
Definition
\[\text{Charm} = \frac{\partial \Delta}{\partial t} = -\frac{\partial \Delta}{\partial T} = \frac{\partial \Theta}{\partial S} = -\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S\,\partial T}\]

Charm is the rate at which Delta bleeds or grows as time passes, even with spot and vol held constant. It is also the spatial derivative of Theta — telling you how Theta changes across different spot prices.

Range
−1 to +1

Positive Charm
ITM calls, OTM puts
Delta grows toward 1 (or 0) as option approaches certainty of exercise
Negative Charm
OTM calls, ITM puts
Delta decays toward 0 as probability of exercise falls with time

Charm Through Time — The Expiry Explosion
100 20%
The Path to Certainty
OTM Options

Delta decays toward 0. The probability of finishing ITM falls with each passing day. The option becomes increasingly worthless. Charm is negative (Delta falling).

ATM Options

Delta gravitates toward ±0.5. This is already the ATM value, so Charm ≈ 0 for options that stay exactly ATM. But the step function becomes sharper — any tiny move creates massive delta change (the Gamma effect).

ITM Options

Delta grows toward ±1. The option increasingly behaves like the underlying. The "uncertainty" about exercise is being resolved daily. Charm is positive for ITM calls (Delta growing).

The Weekend Effect — Interview Favourite
🎯 Interview question: "You are delta-neutral on Friday afternoon. The market doesn't open over the weekend. When you arrive Monday morning with spot unchanged — is your book still delta-neutral?"
If long OTM calls
Delta decayed over weekend
Negative Charm on OTM calls. You are now over-hedged (sold too many shares). Must buy back some stock Monday morning.
If long ITM calls
Delta grew over weekend
Positive Charm on ITM calls. You are now under-hedged. Must sell more stock Monday morning to restore delta-neutrality.

This "time-induced rebalancing" proves that being delta-neutral is a point-in-time statement. Even without any market moves, your hedge ratio changes just from the calendar turning.
